Bauhaus Movement

Sweeney Todd Film Poster

Minimalist Design Geometric Abstraction Color Theory Symbolic Forms

Overview

A film poster study exploring the revolutionary Bauhaus movement's principles of reduction and geometric abstraction. This project demonstrates how to convey complex narratives through minimal design elements.

Applied Bauhaus design principles to create a contemporary film poster for Sweeney Todd, using geometric forms and careful color relationships to tell the story.

The final design uses basic geometric shapes - triangles, circles, and lines - combined with a minimal color palette to represent the story's key elements: the razor blade (triangle), vulnerability (circle), and character identity (diagonal streak).

Key Elements

• Geometric abstraction and minimal forms

• Strategic use of color and transparency

• Focus on essential narrative elements

• True to Bauhaus principles of reduction
