Anointed Gaze

Medium: Ink and Graphite Year: 2022 Dimensions: 8" x 5"

About The Piece

Anointed Gaze emerges from the visual vocabulary of dissociation—that liminal state where consciousness hovers at the periphery of presence. The work began with a deliberately distorted source image, mirroring the perceptual fragmentation that accompanies psychological detachment from immediate reality.

Through stippling and graphite application, the surface develops a granular, oscillating quality that echoes the sensory experience of "zoning out"—that peculiar suspension between awareness and absence. The subject's weighted gaze suggests both burden and beauty within states of mental exhaustion and sensory overload.

Ink and graphite accumulate in deliberate layers, creating textural depth that mirrors how perception can fold inward upon itself. This work functions as visual meditation on burnout's strange aesthetics—finding profound beauty in the moments when the mind seeks refuge elsewhere.
