Digital Texture Experiment

Medium: Motion Design Year: 2025 Software: Adobe After Effects

Concept

This short motion test explores how analog screen artifacts can be recreated procedurally using Adobe After Effects. The goal was to simulate a retro CRT-inspired digital distortion with moving scanlines, pixel breakup, and a subtle neon-green glow—without relying on third-party plugins.

Design Approach

The look is built entirely from native After Effects tools: CC Ball Action, Turbulent Displace, Displacement Map, Wave Warp, Venetian Blinds, Glow, and Flicker Opacity. Multiple distortion systems were layered—pixel breakup, horizontal tearing, wave-based displacement, and directional glow—to create an analog-signal texture that feels alive rather than static. Noise and flicker settings are animated to mimic fluctuating screen output. Color grading pushes the image toward a "cyber green" monochrome palette, reinforcing the degraded digital screen aesthetic.

Skills Demonstrated

  • Procedural motion design without third-party plugins
  • Building coherent visual language using native AE effects
  • Layered displacement workflows
  • Glow, noise, and signal-warping techniques
  • Color-driven mood creation

Result

This technical experiment demonstrates texture, distortion, and atmosphere in motion. It is not tied to a narrative concept—just a test in pushing After Effects to simulate degraded digital screens through procedural techniques.
