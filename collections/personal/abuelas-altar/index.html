Abuela's Altar

Medium: Charcoal Year: 2025 Dimensions: 18" x 24"

About The Piece

“Abuela’s Altar” is a charcoal still life constructed as a personal altar to my late grandmother. It honors the spiritual weight she carried and passed down with all the beauty and fear that came with it.

Each object was carefully chosen and arranged in her memory: a veladora of La Virgen de Guadalupe, a rosary draped over the Death tarot card, a ceramic angel, a funeral cross, and a broken chalice she left behind. The chalice, once hers, is cracked and imperfect, but holds the most meaning. It’s the only physical object I have of hers, and in this drawing, it becomes sacred.

This altar is more than remembrance. It’s a space of reckoning. She taught me to believe in God, but also to fear Him. Drawing this was a way of asking: Is she at peace? Did she find her place in heaven?

Rendered entirely in charcoal, the piece uses contrast and shadow to evoke the intimacy and mystery of memory. Abuela’s Altar is both offering and elegy, a moment of stillness where grief, faith, and love coexist.
