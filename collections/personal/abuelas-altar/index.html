Abuela's Altar

Medium: Charcoal Year: 2025 Dimensions: 18" x 24"

About The Piece

"Abuela's Altar" is a tender yet powerful devotional piece that emerges from the intersection of grief and reverence. Created in charcoal, this work explores the sacred spaces we create to honor those who have passed, blending Catholic and bruja traditions in a way that reflects the complex spiritual landscape of Chicana culture.

Though she's gone, my abuela lingers in rosaries, candles, and the quiet corners of memory. This altar serves as both a prayer for her to reach heaven and a reminder that love survives in every sacred piece she left behind. The careful arrangement of devotional objects speaks to the way we maintain connection with ancestors through ritual and remembrance.

Each mark on paper is an act of devotion, a way of keeping her memory alive through the physical act of creation. This piece blends belief and grief, honoring the complex ways we navigate loss within cultural and spiritual frameworks that shape our understanding of life and death.
