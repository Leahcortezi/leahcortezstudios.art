Inheritance

Medium: Digital Art Year: 2025 Dimensions: 12" x 16"

About The Piece

Inheritance explores cultural identity, generational shame, and the quiet scars of assimilation. Inspired by the self-portraits of Frida Kahlo, the figure appears stripped of traditional markers like tattoos, piercings, and the iconic unibrow—not out of rejection, but reflection. Surrounded by thorned vines and native flora, she exists in a tense stillness—caught between survival and softness.

The piece is rooted in my experience as a “no sabo” kid, raised between cultures. It reflects the emotional weight of being “not enough” on either side and the silence passed down through generations. Through visual symbolism and restraint, Inheritance asks:
If we’ve inherited erasure—can we still claim belonging?
