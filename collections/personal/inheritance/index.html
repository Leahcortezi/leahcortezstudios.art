Inheritance

Medium: Digital Art Year: 2025 Dimensions: 12" x 16"

Project Overview

Inheritance confronts the complex terrain of cultural identity and generational trauma within the Chicana experience, exploring the liminal space of being a "no sabo" kid—raised between worlds, never fully belonging to either.

The Challenge

How do you visually represent the inherited burden of cultural erasure and generational silence? I sought to create a self-portrait that could articulate the quiet violence of assimilation without relying on conventional cultural markers, addressing the question: When erasure becomes legacy, how do we claim our right to belong?

Creative Approach

Drawing inspiration from Frida Kahlo's unflinching self-examination, I deliberately stripped the figure of obvious cultural markers—not through rejection, but to expose the effects of assimilation. The subject is surrounded by thorned native flora, existing in suspended tension between survival and vulnerability, using restrained symbolism and deliberate omissions to speak about identity through absence rather than presence.

Outcome & Reflection

This piece became a breakthrough in my personal work, allowing me to process complex feelings about cultural identity while developing a visual language for discussing generational trauma. It demonstrates my ability to translate deeply personal experiences into universally resonant imagery.
