Entre Mundos

Medium: Colored Pencil and Graphite Year: 2025 Dimensions: 10.5" x 8"

About The Piece

Entre Mundos investigates the symbiotic relationship between mortality and regeneration through botanical-skeletal fusion. This work positions death not as terminus but as generative force—the skull and flower sharing a singular stem in representation of cyclical transformation.

The piece employs contrasting media to reinforce conceptual duality: graphite renders the cranial form in monochromatic stillness, while colored pencil animates the bloom with chromatic vitality. This material distinction emphasizes the eternal dialogue between endings and beginnings, suggesting that beauty and decay occupy the same continuum.

Entre Mundos proposes an ecological philosophy of acceptance—asking what profound growth becomes possible when we learn to hold both flower and bone with equal reverence, recognizing death as life's most generous collaborator.
