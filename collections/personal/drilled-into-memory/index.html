Drilled into Memory

Medium: Graphite Dimensions: 10.5" x 8" Year: 2025

About The Piece

Drilled Into Memory employs an uncomfortably intimate perspective to investigate childhood trauma through the clinical architecture of medical intervention. The composition positions the viewer within the oral cavity, creating an unsettling identification with vulnerability experienced under sedation and harsh institutional lighting.

This deliberate vantage point—simultaneously invasive and protective—has become a recurring motif in my practice, using corporeal interior as both pictorial frame and psychological lens. The body serves as site of memory storage, where traumatic experiences become permanently inscribed within flesh and consciousness.

Through graphite's clinical precision, Drilled Into Memory transforms routine medical procedure into visual archaeology of fear and violation. The work suggests that some memories become so deeply embedded that they reshape our relationship to our own physical boundaries, forever altering how we inhabit our bodies.
