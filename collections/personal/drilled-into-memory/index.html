Drilled into Memory

Medium: Graphite Dimensions: 10.5" x 8" Year: 2025

About The Piece

This drawing reframes a childhood trauma through an unsettling point of view—the back of the throat. The mouth forms a natural frame, placing the viewer in a helpless position I once felt under sedation and dental lights.

That perspective—vulnerable, clinical, and surreal—has stayed with me. I continue to explore variations of it in my work, using the body as both a frame and a lens.

Drilled Into Memory transforms an ordinary dental visit into a visual narrative of fear, intrusion, and memory etched into flesh and paper.
