Echoes of Memoria

Medium: Mixed media, family photographs, acrylic paint

Dimensions: 18" × 24"

Year: 2024

Category: Personal Work

Series: Generational Narratives

Artist Statement

This piece emerges from a deep exploration of family archives and inherited memories. Using found family photographs as a foundation, I layer paint and collage elements to create a visual conversation between past and present generations.

The work investigates how memory transforms over time, becoming both more precious and more fragile. Through selective revelation and concealment of photographic elements, I explore the ways we preserve, reinterpret, and sometimes lose our family stories.

The layered composition reflects the complexity of cultural identity, where individual experiences intersect with collective memory. Each mark and color choice represents an act of remembrance and a gesture toward understanding my place within generational continuity.

Personal Reflection

Creating this piece required courage to work with irreplaceable family photographs. I began by scanning and printing multiple copies, allowing myself the freedom to experiment while preserving the originals.

The painting process became meditative, each brushstroke a conversation with my ancestors. Colors were chosen intuitively, often responding to emotions triggered by specific photographs or memories.

The work evolved over several months, with periods of active creation alternating with contemplative pauses. This rhythm mirrors the way memory itself unfolds - sometimes urgent, sometimes patient.

Cultural Context

This work is part of my ongoing exploration of Chicana identity and the immigrant experience. It speaks to the universal desire to understand our roots while acknowledging the specific cultural forces that shape Mexican-American families.

The piece honors the strength of women in my family who preserved stories and traditions across generations, often in the face of cultural erasure and economic hardship.
