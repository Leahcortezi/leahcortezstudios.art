Anointed Gaze

Medium: Ink and Graphite Year: 2022 Dimensions: 8" x 5"

Project Overview

Anointed Gaze emerges from the visual vocabulary of dissociation—that liminal state where consciousness hovers at the periphery of presence. The work began with a deliberately distorted source image, mirroring the perceptual fragmentation that accompanies psychological detachment from immediate reality.

The Challenge

Visually represent the complex psychological state of dissociation and mental exhaustion through traditional drawing media. Capture the sensory experience of "zoning out" while finding beauty within states of burnout and emotional overwhelm.

Artistic Approach

Through stippling and graphite application, the surface develops a granular, oscillating quality that echoes the sensory experience of dissociation—that peculiar suspension between awareness and absence. Ink and graphite accumulate in deliberate layers, creating textural depth that mirrors how perception can fold inward upon itself during moments of psychological retreat.

Outcome & Meaning

The subject's weighted gaze suggests both burden and beauty within states of mental exhaustion and sensory overload. This work functions as visual meditation on burnout's strange aesthetics—finding profound beauty in the moments when the mind seeks refuge elsewhere, transforming personal psychological experience into universal artistic expression.
