Anointed Gaze

Medium: Ink and Graphite Year: 2022 Dimensions: 8" x 5"

About The Piece

“Anointed Gaze” began as a personal sketch during a quiet moment of dissociation. I was in conversation, but not fully present—zoning out, watching the world blur and soften at the edges. This piece captures that sensation through distortion, texture, and intensity.

I chose stippling to replicate the grainy, vibrating fuzziness that overtakes my vision during moments of fatigue or mental fog. The figure’s eyes are heavy and searching, their gaze exaggerated and unreal. There’s a quiet surrealism in the way the face stretches and leans inward, like a mirror warped by emotion.

Combining ink and graphite allowed me to play with depth and contrast. The stipple texture floats atop a softer graphite base, echoing the layers of perception we experience when our minds drift elsewhere.

“Anointed Gaze” is about presence and absence—being there, but not really. It’s a visual meditation on burnout, overstimulation, and the strange beauty found in the moments when everything goes quiet.
