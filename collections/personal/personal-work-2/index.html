Entre Mundos

Medium: Mixed media, fabric, acrylic paint

Dimensions: 24" × 30"

Year: 2024

Category: Personal Work

Series: Border Crossings

Artist Statement

"Entre Mundos" (Between Worlds) explores the liminal space of cultural identity and the experience of existing between multiple worlds. The work incorporates fabric from traditional Mexican textiles alongside contemporary painting techniques, creating a dialogue between past and present.

The composition reflects the complexity of navigating different cultural contexts while maintaining a sense of authentic self. Symbolic elements reference both Mexican folklore and contemporary American experience, highlighting the rich complexity of bicultural identity.

Through layered imagery and mixed media techniques, the piece invites viewers to consider their own experiences of belonging and displacement, recognizing that identity is often found in the spaces between defined categories.

Creative Journey

This piece began during a period of personal reflection on family migration stories and my own sense of place within multiple cultural contexts. I gathered fabrics from family textiles and incorporated them as both literal and metaphorical elements.

The painting process involved building up layers gradually, allowing each element to inform the next. Traditional Mexican color palettes were juxtaposed with more muted contemporary tones, creating visual tension that reflects cultural complexity.

Working with family textiles required careful consideration of preservation and respect for cultural heritage while pushing these materials into new artistic contexts.

Cultural Resonance

This work is part of my ongoing exploration of Chicana identity and the immigrant experience in contemporary America. It speaks to the beauty and challenge of maintaining cultural connections while adapting to new environments.

The piece honors the resilience of immigrant communities while acknowledging the emotional complexity of cultural transition. It celebrates the richness that comes from living between worlds while recognizing the challenges this positioning can present.

Through personal narrative, the work connects to broader themes of displacement, adaptation, and cultural preservation that resonate across many communities.
