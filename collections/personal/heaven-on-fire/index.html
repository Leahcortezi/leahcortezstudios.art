Heaven on Fire

Medium: Mixed Media Year: 2021 Dimensions: 30" x 40"

About The Piece

"Heaven on Fire" presents an abstract vision of paradise unraveling—flames licking the clouds, chaos blooming in what was once sacred ground. Created in 2021 during a period of global upheaval and personal transformation, this mixed media piece captures the sensation of watching certainties dissolve and familiar structures burn away.

The work explores themes of disillusionment and spiritual reckoning, questioning what happens when the promises of paradise fail to materialize or reveal themselves to be built on unstable foundations. Through layered textures and explosive color, the piece visualizes the moment when faith itself becomes a battleground.

Yet within this destruction lies the possibility of transformation. The fire that consumes also purifies, clearing space for new growth and understanding. "Heaven on Fire" reflects on the necessary destruction that sometimes precedes spiritual evolution—the burning away of illusions to reveal deeper truths. It speaks to anyone who has watched their belief systems crumble and found themselves stronger in the rebuilding.

The mixed media approach allows for both controlled and chaotic elements, mirroring the way destruction and creation intertwine. Layers of paint, collage, and texture create a visual language that speaks to the complexity of spiritual crisis and renewal.
