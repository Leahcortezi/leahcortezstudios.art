Heaven on Fire

Medium: Mixed media

Dimensions: 8" × 10"

Year: 2021

Project Overview

Heaven on Fire envisions paradise in a state of beautiful collapse—a surreal landscape where divine order gives way to sublime chaos. This mixed-media composition layers colored pencil, watercolor, and ink to create a world balanced on the precipice between transcendence and destruction.

The Challenge

Create a visual interpretation of paradise that challenges traditional representations, exploring the tension between transcendence and destruction while developing a personal visual language for spiritual and emotional transformation.

Artistic Approach

The work employs intuitive color relationships and gestural mark-making to build what might be called "structured randomness"—deliberate spontaneity that mirrors the unpredictable nature of spiritual transformation. Each hue was selected through a dialogue between instinct and intention, creating harmonious discord within the pictorial space through layered mixed-media techniques.

Outcome & Meaning

The resulting dreamscape suggests a realm where beauty and terror coexist, where the familiar boundaries between salvation and damnation dissolve into something entirely new. It is paradise reimagined—not as eternal perfection, but as constant, magnificent becoming, challenging viewers to reconsider their assumptions about divine spaces and spiritual experiences.
