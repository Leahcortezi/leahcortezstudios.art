Heaven on Fire

Medium: Mixed media

Dimensions: 8" × 10"

Year: 2021

About The Piece

“Heaven on Fire” is a surreal landscape where utopia and dystopia collide. It imagines a world that is blooming and burning at the same time—alive with vibrant textures, unnatural colors, and shifting forms.

Made with colored pencil, watercolor, and ink, this piece layers mark-making to build a chaotic beauty. Bright greens and sunny yellows melt into scorched reds and unnatural purples. Sunlight breaks through clouds like stained glass, while fire creeps across the edges of paradise.

The name Heaven on Fire holds a deeper meaning: it’s about how even the most sacred places can feel dangerous, overwhelming, or on the verge of collapse. It’s the emotional dissonance of existing between peace and panic, beauty and fear. Sometimes destruction grows from the same root as creation.

Heaven on Fire isn’t a warning or a prediction—it’s a reflection of right now. Where joy and fear coexist. Where something beautiful can still burn.
