Heaven on Fire

Medium: Mixed media

Dimensions: 30" × 40"

Year: 2021

Category: Personal Work

Series: Spiritual Landscapes

About The Piece

“Heaven on Fire” is a surreal landscape where utopia and dystopia collide. It imagines a world that is burning and blooming at the same time, abstracted into vibrant textures, unnatural colors, and shifting forms.

Created with colored pencil, watercolor, and ink, this piece uses layered mark-making to build a chaotic harmony. Bright tropical greens and soft blues sit beside scorched reds and unnatural purples. Sunlight breaks through clouds like stained glass, while fire creeps from the edges of paradise. At the center, a single red figure is suspended midfall or midflight.

This work reflects the emotional dissonance of existing between peace and panic, beauty and danger. It’s about how even the most sacred spaces can feel overwhelming, and how destruction can hide inside something that once felt divine.

“Heaven on Fire” is not a warning or a vision of the future—it’s a reflection of now, where joy and fear often grow from the same root.
