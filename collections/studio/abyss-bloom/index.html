Abyss Bloom

Medium: Wire armature, stretched nylon, glue, iridescent paints Dimensions: 3.3" × 7" × 3" Year: 2025

About The Piece

This piece began as a study in form, constructed with wire, stretched nylon, and layered shapes. The final result—a twisting, alien spiral—took on new meaning as I stepped back and studied it.

It reminded me of deep sea creatures and Cronenbergian forms: organic, unsettling, beautiful. I leaned into that vision, painting the surface with color-shifting iridescent pigments and glow-in-the-dark accents, echoing the bioluminescence of an underwater alien bloom.

Abyss Bloom exists in the in-between—sculpture and specimen, elegant and eerie, grounded in process but blooming from pure imagination.
