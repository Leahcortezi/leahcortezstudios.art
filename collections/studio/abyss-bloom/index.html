Abyss Bloom

Medium: Wire armature, stretched nylon, glue

Dimensions: 3.3" × 7" × 3"

Year: 2025

About This Piece

“Abyss Bloom” began as a study in form and shape for a Design II course, constructed using wire armature, stretched nylon, and layers of glue for structure. The result was an unexpected spiral—a twisting, organic square crowned by a jagged starburst.

Though it was originally designed from abstract intuition, the final form reminded me of deep sea creatures—those bizarre and beautiful beings that thrive in darkness. Inspired by that connection, I finished the surface with color-shifting iridescent paints and glow-in-the-dark details that trace along the edges and crown, echoing the bioluminescence of abyssal lifeforms.

This piece invites viewers to see a sculpture not just as object, but as presence. It shifts and shimmers depending on the light, evoking something alive—strange, elegant, and possibly still evolving.

“Abyss Bloom” is a creature of the in-between: sculpture and specimen, organic and invented, grounded in process but blooming from imagination.
