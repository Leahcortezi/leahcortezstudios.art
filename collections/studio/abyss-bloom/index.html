Abyss Bloom

Medium: Wire armature, stretched nylon, glue, iridescent paints Dimensions: 3.3" × 7" × 3" Year: 2025

Project Overview

This piece began as a study in form, constructed with wire, stretched nylon, and layered shapes. The final result—a twisting, alien spiral—took on new meaning as I stepped back and studied it.

The Challenge

Explore three-dimensional form through experimental material manipulation, creating a sculptural piece that pushes beyond traditional boundaries while discovering unexpected meanings through the creative process.

Creative Solution

Using wire armature as foundation, I stretched nylon and built layered shapes, allowing the form to evolve organically. When the twisted, alien spiral emerged, I recognized its resemblance to deep sea creatures and Cronenbergian forms—organic, unsettling, beautiful. I embraced this vision, painting the surface with color-shifting iridescent pigments and glow-in-the-dark accents to echo the bioluminescence of an underwater alien bloom.

Outcome & Reflection

Abyss Bloom exists in the in-between—sculpture and specimen, elegant and eerie, grounded in process but blooming from pure imagination. The piece demonstrates how experimental approaches can lead to unexpected discoveries, revealing new meanings that emerge through intuitive making and thoughtful observation.
