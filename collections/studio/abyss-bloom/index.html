Abyss Bloom

Medium: Wire armature, stretched nylon, glue, iridescent paints Dimensions: 3.3" × 7" × 3" Year: 2025

Project Overview

Abyss Bloom explores the transformation of flat surfaces into three-dimensional sculptural forms. This piece investigates how bending and rotating translucent materials with linear wire structures can create biomorphic shapes that blur the line between organic and constructed forms.

The Challenge

Play with surfaces, bending and rotating them with lines into a 3D shape. The work needed to explore how flat materials could be manipulated through linear structures to create dimensional forms with organic qualities.

Creative Solution

Starting with wire and stretched nylon, I shaped organic forms through layering and twisting. As the piece developed, a spiral form emerged—reminiscent of sea creatures and alien botanicals. I embraced this unsettling growth and painted the surface with iridescent pigments and glow-in-the-dark elements to mimic bioluminescence. The form became both bloom and specimen, a beautiful and eerie organism.

Outcome & Reflection

Abyss Bloom lives in the liminal space between sculpture and lifeform. It demonstrates how intuitive material play can generate new meaning—where imagination, process, and form collide. This piece reflects my exploration of the uncanny in nature, and how unfamiliar beauty can emerge from experimental practice.
