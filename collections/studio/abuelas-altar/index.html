Abuela's Altar

Medium: Charcoal Year: 2025 Dimensions: 18" x 24"

Project Overview

Abuela's Altar is a charcoal still life that honors both the beauty of Catholic devotion and the grief of ancestral loss. This piece explores the inherited tension between organized tradition and personal spirituality, reflecting on generational complexities of fear, faith, and identity passed down from my grandmother.

The Challenge

Create a still life composition that honors both the beauty of Catholic devotion and the grief of ancestral loss. The project needed to visually express the inherited tension between organized tradition and personal spirituality while reflecting on the generational complexities of fear, faith, and identity passed down from my grandmother.

Creative Solution

Inspired by Churrigueresque altar design, I curated a still life composed of prayer candles, tarot cards, and fragmented relics from my grandmother's life. These objects served as symbolic anchors of memory and ritual. I used charcoal to emphasize shadow and light, reflecting the flickering and ephemeral nature of remembrance. The medium's high contrast mirrored the emotional weight of mourning, allowing the composition to feel both intimate and sacred.

Outcome & Impact

This piece bridges technical still life drawing with cultural storytelling, using academic skill to honor personal heritage. By transforming keepsakes into relics, the altar becomes a visual elegy—a contemporary sacred space grounded in intergenerational memory. It became a foundational work in exploring how cultural identity, spirituality, and grief converge in my art practice.
