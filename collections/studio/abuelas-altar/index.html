Abuela's Altar

Medium: Charcoal Year: 2025 Dimensions: 18" x 24"

Project Overview

Abuela's Altar merges Catholic devotion with ancestral memory through the intimate language of charcoal, transforming personal objects into sacred relics that preserve my grandmother Irma's presence.

The Challenge

Create a still life composition that could honor both the complexity of faith and the reality of loss while exploring the tension between Catholic tradition and personal spiritual practice. The work needed to capture the dual inheritance of fear and faith that my grandmother carried.

Creative Approach

Inspired by the ornate complexity of Churrigueresque altars, I wove together prayer candles, tarot cards, and fragmented keepsakes to create a visual dialogue between faith and intuition. Using charcoal's natural capacity for deep contrasts, I echoed the shadows and light that define memory itself, allowing the medium to reinforce the thematic content.

Outcome & Impact

This piece successfully bridged traditional still life techniques with deeply personal narrative, demonstrating my ability to use academic skills in service of meaningful content. It became a cornerstone piece for understanding how cultural heritage can inform contemporary art practice.
