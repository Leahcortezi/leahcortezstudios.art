Color Studies & Process

Rhythmic Intervals

Medium: Acrylic on wood panel

Dimensions: 20" × 20"

Year: 2024

Category: Studio Work

Series: Geometric Meditations

Artist Statement

This square composition explores the relationship between geometric structure and organic rhythm. Through careful color placement and proportional relationships, I investigate how mathematical precision can express emotional content.

The work draws inspiration from musical composition, particularly the concept of intervals and their harmonic relationships. Each color area functions as a note in a visual symphony, creating tension and resolution through proximity and contrast.

By constraining myself to a square format and geometric forms, I push against limitations to discover new possibilities for expression. The resulting composition balances order with spontaneity, structure with feeling.

Creative Process

The composition began with extensive color studies on paper, exploring relationships between warm and cool tones. I created multiple small-scale versions before committing to the final large format.

The wood panel was carefully prepared with multiple coats of gesso to achieve a smooth, even surface. Each color area was painted wet-on-dry to maintain crisp edges and pure color saturation.

The work required patience and precision, with multiple sessions dedicated to achieving the exact color relationships envisioned in the preliminary studies.

Artistic Influences

This piece draws from the traditions of hard-edge painting and color field work, while incorporating contemporary approaches to geometric abstraction. The influence of Josef Albers' color theory is evident in the careful attention to color interaction.

The work also reflects my interest in the mathematical principles underlying natural forms, finding beauty in the intersection of logic and intuition.
