Gnaw

Medium: Plaster Year: 2025 Dimensions: 4" x 6" x 4"

About The Piece

“Gnaw” was created for a Design II project that explored themes of subtraction, bodily texture, and transformation through the subtractive sculpting process of plaster carving. Beginning with a cast cylinder, I gradually reshaped the material using chisels and rasps, intuitively carving away at the form until it evoked a new, unsettling structure—one inspired by the mouth, teeth, and tongue, but intentionally abstracted and reimagined.

Rather than aiming for anatomical realism, I sought to deconstruct the human mouth into something more grotesque and creature-like. The exaggerated gesture of the tongue curls skyward, while the hollow, cavity-like lower structure mimics a molar worn down by stress or decay. These distortions reflect the psychological tension of being consumed or eroded from within—an object that feels chewed, gnawed, and spiritually excavated.

The matte white surface and raw, carved texture emphasize the fossil-like, almost sacred materiality of the piece. “Gnaw” occupies the ambiguous space between relic and body—caught between the figurative and the fragmented. It reads as a symbol of hunger, silence, internalized violence, or unspoken emotion: a relic of speech turned into sculpture.

By reducing the body to a visceral and minimal core, I invite viewers to question where the line is drawn between form and feeling, language and flesh, figure and fossil. “Gnaw” is less about representation and more about transformation—of material, of meaning, and of memory etched into physical space.
