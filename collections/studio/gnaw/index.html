Gnaw

Medium: Plaster Year: 2025 Dimensions: 4" x 6" x 4"

Project Overview

"Gnaw" was created for a Design II project exploring subtraction, texture, and transformation through plaster carving. Starting with a cast cylinder, I used chisels and rasps to slowly reveal a new form—one inspired by the mouth, teeth, and tongue, but intentionally distorted and reimagined.

The Challenge

Explore sculptural subtraction as a creative methodology, transforming a simple plaster cylinder into an expressive form that captures both anatomical reference and emotional metaphor while demonstrating advanced carving techniques and surface treatment.

Creative Solution

Rather than depicting anatomical accuracy, I aimed to deconstruct the mouth into something more abstract and creature-like. The gesture of the tongue curls upward, while the lower structure mimics a row of simplified molars—evoking both the physical act of biting and the emotional tension of being chewed on from the inside. The matte white surface and carved texture heighten the raw, almost fossilized quality.

Outcome & Reflection

"Gnaw" lives in the space between figure and fragment—a relic of hunger, language, and the body reinterpreted through form. The piece demonstrates how subtractive processes can reveal unexpected meanings, where the act of carving away material becomes a metaphor for uncovering hidden emotional states and primal experiences.
