Gnaw

Medium: Plaster Year: 2025 Dimensions: 4" x 6" x 4"

Project Overview

Gnaw is a carved plaster sculpture that explores transformation through destruction. Using subtractive carving techniques, this piece investigates how the act of removing material can expose deeper meaning, drawing on anatomical reference as metaphor for internal erosion and unspoken pain.

The Challenge

Translate a cast plaster cylinder into an emotionally resonant sculpture using subtractive carving. The work needed to explore transformation through destruction—how the act of carving away material can expose meaning—while drawing on anatomical reference as metaphor.

Creative Approach

Rather than depict anatomical accuracy, I deconstructed the mouth into a distorted, creature-like form. The tongue curls upward, while the lower structure suggests a jagged row of molars, evoking a tension between external aggression and internal erosion. The raw plaster surface, rough chisel marks, and bone-white coloration enhanced the sense of something chewed from the inside out—haunted, fossilized, and unspoken.

Outcome & Reflection

Gnaw lives in the space between figure and fragment. It speaks to hunger, anxiety, and language through the act of subtraction. The process of carving away material revealed a deeper emotional state, turning the sculpture into a metaphor for unspoken pain and the primal need to make meaning out of erosion.
