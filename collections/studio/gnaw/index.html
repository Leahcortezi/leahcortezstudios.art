Gnaw

Medium: Plaster sculpture Year: 2024

About The Piece

Gnaw manifests as a sculptural investigation into corporeal transformation, where the body becomes site of simultaneous creation and destruction. Through subtractive carving processes, plaster yields to reveal forms that suggest anatomical memory while refusing literal representation.

The work presents a hollowed form punctuated by a circular aperture and eroded base—familiar bodily markers distorted beyond recognition into something archaeological, fossilized. These exaggerated anatomical cues operate not as mimesis but as metaphor, allowing internal tensions and psychological decay to assume physical materiality.

Gnaw proposes a meditation on consumption as cyclical process—where the body functions simultaneously as consumer and consumed, agent and object of its own transformation. The sculpture embodies the paradox of preservation through erosion, suggesting that sometimes what remains after destruction may be more truthful than what came before.
