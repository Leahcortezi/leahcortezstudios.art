Gnaw

Medium: Plaster Year: 2025 Dimensions: 4" x 6" x 4"

About The Piece

This plaster sculpture explores themes of bodily texture, decay, and transformation through carving. I reimagined the body through exaggerated anatomical cues.

The hollowed-out form, circular “mouth,” and gnawed base distort familiar features into something fossilized and surreal. It’s not about realism, but about decay, memory, and internal tension taking physical shape.

Gnaw is a meditation on consumption and erosion—where the body is both devourer and devoured.
