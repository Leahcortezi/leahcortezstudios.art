Feathers Along the Bend

Medium: Clay Mold Making and Cement Casting

Dimensions: 8" × 8" × 2"

Year: 2025

Project Overview

Feathers Along the Bend is a cement relief sculpture created through subtractive carving, mold-making, and casting for a public botanical garden installation. This piece honors local South Texas bird species while demonstrating advanced casting techniques suitable for outdoor environments.

The Challenge

Design a durable outdoor relief sculpture that honors local South Texas bird species while developing advanced casting and mold-making techniques suitable for botanical garden installation.

Creative Solution

Using subtractive carving and mold-making methods, I created bird forms inspired by the Blue Heron, Royal Tern, and Double-Crested Cormorant. These silhouettes were arranged to evoke the fluid motion of flight, grounded in the cement's surface texture. I embedded rhythms of water and stillness into the relief to reflect the birds' natural environment, blending with the garden's landscape.

Outcome & Impact

This piece pays tribute to Corpus Christi's birdwatching culture and coastal ecosystem. By combining ecological observation with sculptural discipline, Feathers Along the Bend honors the poetic act of noticing—and invites viewers into a moment of quiet reverence for the overlooked beauty of native wildlife.
