Feathers Along the Bend

Medium: Clay Mold Making and Cement Casting

Dimensions: 8" × 8" × 2"

Year: 2025

About The Piece

“Feathers Along the Bend” was created for a Design II project exploring subtractive sculpting and public art. The process involved carving a design into clay, creating a plaster mold, and casting the final piece in cement to be installed as part of a botanical garden exhibition.

This relief features three bird species native to the Corpus Christi region: the elegant Blue Heron, the sharp-beaked Royal Tern, and the striking Double-Crested Cormorant. These species were chosen for their unique silhouettes and symbolic presence in the local landscape, reflecting the passion and patience of South Texas birdwatchers.

The carved textures and flowing lines are meant to evoke both the movement of water and the serenity of still observation. The cement form is designed to weather naturally outdoors, grounding the artwork in the same environment that inspired it.

“Feathers Along the Bend” is a tribute to coastal ecology, migratory memory, and the beauty of paying attention to the life around us.
