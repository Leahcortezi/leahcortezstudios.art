Feathers Along the Bend

Medium: Clay Mold Making and Cement Casting

Dimensions: 8" × 8" × 2"

Year: 2025

About The Piece

This cement relief sculpture was created through subtractive carving, mold-making, and casting for a public botanical garden installation. Inspired by the birds of Corpus Christi, I chose native species—the Blue Heron, Royal Tern, and Double-Crested Cormorant—for their striking silhouettes and symbolic ties to migration and memory.

The flowing lines and textures evoke both water and stillness—mirroring the rhythm of flight and the quiet focus of observation. The weathered cement allows the piece to blend with the natural environment that inspired it.

Feathers Along the Bend is a tribute to South Texas birdwatchers, coastal ecosystems, and the beauty of noticing the life that surrounds us.
