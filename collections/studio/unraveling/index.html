Unraveling

Medium: Wire and Wood Year: 2025 Dimensions: 9" x 4.5" x 15"

About The Piece

This wire self-portrait explores how line can exist in three dimensions—bending perspective into form. I used gesture, not realism, to express emotional exhaustion and inner chaos.

Swirls in the eyes reflect dissociation, while the wire tightly wraps around the face like pressure building inward. At the top, the lines unravel—rising loose and frayed, releasing tension. The wooden base grounds the floating form, adding warmth beneath the instability.

Unraveling is about giving emotion physical shape—letting line twist, tighten, and come undone.
