Unraveling

Medium: Wire and Wood Year: 2025 Dimensions: 9" x 4.5" x 15"

Project Overview

Unraveling reimagines the concept of portraiture using linear sculpture. Using only wire, this three-dimensional self-portrait externalizes internal emotion, rejecting anatomical likeness in favor of expressive abstraction that transforms psychological states into physical form.

The Challenge

Reimagine the concept of portraiture using linear sculpture. Using only wire, create a three-dimensional form that externalizes internal emotion, rejecting anatomical likeness in favor of expressive abstraction.

Creative Solution

I sculpted a self-portrait from tightly wound and frayed wire, focusing on emotional gesture rather than facial features. The spiraling forms around the eyes evoke visual distortion, while the pressure of wrapped wire around facial planes suggests internalized stress. Ascending lines crown the form, reaching upward as if releasing tension into space. The wire's instability became part of the message—expressing fragmentation, fragility, and movement.

Outcome & Innovation

This piece proposes that line, usually confined to the page, can act as psychological structure in space. It blends drawing and sculpture, making abstract emotion feel bodily and physical. Unraveling opens a new dimension of expression where the act of building becomes an act of emotional release.
