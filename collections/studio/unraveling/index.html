Unraveling

Medium: Wire and Wood Year: 2025 Dimensions: 9" x 4.5" x 15"

About The Piece

Unraveling translates the conventions of portraiture into three-dimensional linear language, where wire becomes both medium and metaphor for psychological states. This sculptural self-portrait abandons mimetic representation in favor of gestural expression, allowing emotion to dictate form rather than anatomical accuracy.

Spiraling elements around the ocular regions suggest the visual distortions of dissociation, while tightly wound wire compresses around facial planes like externalized pressure. The ascending lines that crown the composition perform a deliberate release—rising loose and frayed in visual representation of tension's dissolution. The wooden base provides material grounding, its warmth contrasting with the metal's instability.

This work proposes that line—traditionally confined to paper's surface—can inhabit space as psychological architecture, demonstrating how abstract emotion can assume concrete physical presence through sculptural transformation.
