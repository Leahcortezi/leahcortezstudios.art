Still Arrangement

Medium: Charcoal Year: 2025 Dimensions: 22" x 30"

About The Piece

Still Arrangement is a charcoal value study created for a drawing course focused on mastering local value—learning to push true black, pure white, and everything in between.

The still life features a carefully arranged set of objects: a matte black deer sculpture, a box-shaped container holding artificial flowers, a bell pepper, and a glass goblet. Each item was chosen for its unique texture and tonal challenge, allowing me to practice translating form and contrast using only charcoal.

At the time, I hadn’t yet learned how to draw glass, but this piece still reflects a turning point in my drawing practice. I focused on achieving depth through shadows and highlights, gradually building up confidence with tone and structure.

Still Arrangement documents my progress with charcoal and shows how technical exercises can reveal early glimpses of personal style.
