Weight of Light

Medium: Oil and charcoal on canvas

Dimensions: 36" × 48"

Year: 2024

Category: Studio Work

Series: Light Studies

Artist Statement

This large-scale work investigates the ephemeral qualities of light as it moves through space and time. Using gestural mark-making and atmospheric color techniques, I capture moments of luminosity that exist between observation and memory.

The composition emerges from a series of plein air studies conducted during different times of day, focusing on how light transforms our perception of space. The resulting work abstracts these observations into an emotional landscape of color and movement.

Through the interplay of oil paint and charcoal, I create passages that oscillate between representation and pure abstraction, inviting viewers to experience their own relationship with light and atmosphere.

Technical Approach

The work began with extensive charcoal drawings created en plein air, capturing the essential structures and movements of light. These drawings served as the foundation for the larger canvas work.

Oil paint was applied in both thin glazes and thick impasto, creating varied textural surfaces that respond differently to light. The interplay between transparent and opaque passages mirrors the way light itself behaves in natural environments.

The painting process required working quickly to capture the energy of the initial observations while allowing for careful consideration of color relationships and atmospheric effects.
