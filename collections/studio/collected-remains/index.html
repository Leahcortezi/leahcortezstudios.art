Collected Remains

Medium: Charcoal Dimensions: 24" × 36" Year: 2025

About The Piece

“Collected Remains” was created for a drawing assignment that asked us to collect found objects from around the studio and build a still life from them. Instead of drawing a single scene, I approached the task like a collage—selecting, rearranging, and composing individual items into a carefully balanced drawing that wouldn’t exist in real life, but felt grounded in form and texture.

The objects—a dried plant bundle, pear, pinecone, berries, and a glass bottle—were chosen for their dramatic variation in surface and density. I composed them with intention, letting each object lead into the next visually, creating a gentle rhythm of curves, spikes, and shadows.

Using charcoal, I emphasized the unique qualities of each material: the layered roughness of the pinecone, the softness of the fruit, and the shine and transparency of the bottle. Though all rendered in a monochromatic palette, the piece depends entirely on texture and contrast to feel dimensional and tactile.

“Collected Remains” is both an observational drawing and a constructed world—a quiet arrangement of forgotten or natural things made significant through selection, placement, and the hand that rendered them.
