Collected Remains

Medium: Charcoal Year: 2025 Dimensions: 24" × 36"

Project Overview

Collected Remains transforms discarded studio objects into a meditation on memory and material significance. This charcoal composition approaches still life as constructed narrative, where found objects become protagonists in a carefully orchestrated visual story about what we preserve and what we leave behind.

The Challenge

Create a still life composition from found objects around the studio, but approach the assignment as visual archaeology rather than traditional observational drawing. Transform forgotten materials into meaningful artistic statement through selection, arrangement, and rendering technique.

Creative Solution

Rather than drawing a single scene, I approached the task like a collage—selecting, rearranging, and composing individual items into a carefully balanced drawing that wouldn't exist in real life, but felt grounded in form and texture. The objects—dried plant bundle, pear, pinecone, berries, and glass bottle—were chosen for their dramatic variation in surface and density, creating a gentle rhythm of curves, spikes, and shadows.

Outcome & Impact

Collected Remains functions as both observational drawing and constructed world—a quiet arrangement of forgotten or natural things made significant through selection, placement, and the hand that rendered them. The work demonstrates how traditional drawing techniques can transform mundane materials into meaningful artistic statements about memory, preservation, and the stories objects carry.
