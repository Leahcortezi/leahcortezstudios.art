Artificial Meadow

Medium: Colored Pencil Year: 2025 Dimensions: 9" x 12"

About The Piece

Artificial Meadow orchestrates a visual metamorphosis, transforming mundane household objects into an enchanted topography. Through deliberate misreading, a textile scarf becomes undulating grassland, its form echoing the whimsical linearity found in Dr. Seuss's illustrations, while crystalline fragments punctuate the composition with angular interruptions that disrupt the organic flow.

Executed in colored pencil, the work employs saturated pigmentation and gestural layering to negotiate the boundary between the natural and constructed. The atmospheric sky and luminous terrain construct an invitation into liminal space—where perceptual transformation becomes both subject and methodology.

This piece investigates the cognitive act of reimagining: how the simple recontextualization of familiar materials can generate new meaning, suggesting that the process of creative reconstruction may be as significant as the final visual outcome.
