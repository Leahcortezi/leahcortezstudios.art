Weight of Light

Medium: Charcoal

Dimensions: 22" × 30"

Year: 2025

About The Piece

Weight of Light is my first still life created in drawing class—a foundational piece that introduced me to charcoal and the art of observing light. The setup featured a direct light source casting sharp highlights and deep shadows across a simple arrangement: a sphere, a rectangular block, and flowers tucked into the foreground.

While this piece doesn’t yet show the full range of my capabilities, it represents the beginning of a process that would later define my drawing style. I focused on translating contrast and capturing the way light bounces, pools, and defines form. The sphere became a study in curvature and core shadow, while the rectangular prism taught me to work with planes and directional light.

Weight of Light holds personal value—not because of its complexity, but because of its role in teaching me to see. Every blend, shadow, and edge was part of learning how to sculpt with tone.
