About The Piece

This charcoal investigation of drapery represents a pivotal evolution in observational methodology—a transition from linear documentation to volumetric construction through tonal architecture. The work employs systematic chiaroscuro techniques to reveal how light functions as both material and sculptor, carving dimensional reality from two-dimensional surface.

Through iterative mark-making processes, each charcoal application builds atmospheric density and tactile presence, transforming the fabric's temporal folds into permanent spatial relationships. The repetitive nature of shadow patterns creates rhythmic visual dialogue, establishing meditative drawing practice as both phenomenological investigation and material exploration.

This work transcends traditional fabric study, functioning as an extended meditation on perceptual depth—an exercise in sustained observation that reveals the sculptural potential inherent in drawing's temporal unfolding.

Shadows in Repetition

Medium: Charcoal

Dimensions: 22" × 30"

Year: 2025

About The Piece

This charcoal fabric study marked a turning point in my drawing practice—where I stopped just outlining form and started sculpting with shadow.

Using a draped cloth, I studied how light pools, fades, and repeats across folds. Each pass of charcoal became a meditative process of building depth through texture and contrast. I approached it like a sculptor, not just a drawer.

Shadows in Repetition is more than a study of fabric. It’s about learning to see—deeply, patiently, and with care.
