Shadows in Repetition

Medium: Acrylic on wood panel

Dimensions: 20" × 20"

Year: 2024

Category: Studio Work

Series: Geometric Meditations

About The Piece

“Shadows in Repetition” is a fabric study created for a drawing course focused on shading and rendering form through value. This was one of my earliest charcoal pieces, and it marked a major turning point in how I approached drawing—not as just lines on paper, but as sculpting with shadow.

The piece captures a draped cloth arranged to create folds, dips, and rhythmic light patterns. I approached it like a sculptor, slowly adding and subtracting charcoal to shape the illusion of depth. Blending became a meditative process—carving form out of flatness by paying close attention to how light pools, fades, and clings to texture.

This drawing was a foundational moment for me, where the act of observation became physical and immersive. “Shadows in Repetition” isn’t just about fabric—it’s about learning to see.
