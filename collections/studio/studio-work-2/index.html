Studio Work 2

Medium: Acrylic and charcoal on canvas

Dimensions: 30" × 40"

Year: 2024

Category: Studio Work

Artist Statement

This vertical composition explores the relationship between urban environments and emotional landscapes. Through gestural mark-making and atmospheric color fields, I investigate how physical spaces influence our internal experiences.

The work draws inspiration from early morning walks through the city, capturing the interplay of light and shadow, movement and stillness. Layers of transparent and opaque paint create depth that mirrors the complexity of urban experience.

By abstracting architectural forms and natural elements, the piece invites contemplation on the boundary between constructed and organic environments, questioning how we navigate both external and internal geographies.

Process

The composition began with loose charcoal drawings, establishing vertical rhythms that echo urban architecture. Multiple thin glazes of acrylic paint were built up gradually, allowing each layer to inform the next.

The work evolved through a process of addition and subtraction, with areas scraped back to reveal underlying layers. This archaeological approach to painting reflects the layered history of urban spaces.
