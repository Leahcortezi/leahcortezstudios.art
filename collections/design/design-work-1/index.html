Design Work 1

Medium: Digital design, print applications

Project Type: Visual identity system

Year: 2024

Category: Design Work

Client: Cultural arts organization

Project Overview

This comprehensive visual identity system was developed for a cultural arts organization dedicated to preserving and celebrating Chicana heritage. The design approach balances traditional cultural elements with contemporary design principles.

The identity system encompasses logo design, color palette development, typography selection, and application guidelines across both digital and print mediums. Special attention was given to cultural sensitivity and authentic representation.

The final system successfully bridges generational perspectives, creating a visual language that resonates with both traditional community members and younger audiences seeking cultural connection.

Design Process

The project began with extensive community research and stakeholder interviews to understand cultural values and visual preferences. Mood boards were developed incorporating traditional motifs, contemporary design trends, and symbolic elements.

Multiple concept directions were explored, with iterative feedback sessions ensuring cultural authenticity. The final design integrates botanical elements inspired by traditional artwork with clean, modern typography.

Applications were tested across various mediums to ensure consistency and impact, from large-scale banners to small digital icons.

Technical Specifications

Color Palette: Deep burgundy, warm terracotta, sage green, cream

Typography: Custom modified serif for headings, clean sans-serif for body text

Applications: Business cards, letterhead, website, social media, event materials

File Formats: Vector (AI, EPS), raster (PNG, JPG), web-optimized
