Psychedelic Movement

Project: Concert Poster Year: 2025 Medium: Digital Design Size: 11" × 17" poster

Concept

This concert poster channels the psychedelic movement's visual language to capture Kali Uchis's dreamy, boundary-pushing aesthetic. The design explores neo-psychedelia through vibrant color relationships, flowing organic forms, and surreal imagery that evokes dream states and altered perception. The goal was to create a poster that functions as both promotional material and visual experience—inviting viewers into a hypnotic, otherworldly atmosphere.

Design Approach

Drawing from 1960s psychedelic poster traditions—particularly the San Francisco rock poster movement and artists like Wes Wilson and Victor Moscoso—the design employs saturated, high-contrast color palettes that vibrate optically. Flowing, organic typography merges with figurative elements, blurring the line between text and image. Gradients and color transitions create depth and movement, while the central figure emerges from layers of swirling forms that suggest both cosmic expansion and intimate introspection.

Result

The finished poster successfully translates psychedelic visual principles into a contemporary context while honoring the movement's legacy of using design to evoke altered states of consciousness. The work demonstrates how historical design movements can be reinterpreted to create fresh, culturally relevant visual communication that captures an artist's essence through purely visual means.
