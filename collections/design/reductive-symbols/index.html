Reductive Symbols

Medium: Print design, typography Year: 2024 Pages: 128 pages

About The Piece

“Apology” is a reductive typographic symbol created to visually interpret the emotional weight of the word through form. The goal of this project was to simplify a single letterform into a visual metaphor that expands the word’s meaning.

Starting with a lowercase “n”, I reshaped the letter into a bowed human-like figure—its head lowered, shoulders slumped—to convey vulnerability, humility, and sincerity. The tulip was added as a soft, symbolic gesture of apology and emotional outreach.

Every extension of the form was designed with respect to the original letter’s proportions, referencing the same curvature and stroke weight used by the typeface designer. This helped ground the emotional symbol in recognizable visual language while keeping the abstraction subtle and cohesive.

“Apology” becomes more than just a word—it’s a posture, a feeling, a quiet gesture made visible through type.
