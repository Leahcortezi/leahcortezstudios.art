About The Piece

Reductive Symbols transforms the lowercase "n" into an embodied expression of contrition, wherein letterform becomes human gesture. The character's morphology suggests a bowed figure—its curved spine conveying vulnerability, humility, and the physical manifestation of remorse.

The accompanying tulip functions as both compositional counterpoint and symbolic extension, introducing themes of renewal and gentle offering. Each typographic decision preserves the structural integrity of the original character while expanding its semantic potential, demonstrating how reductive design can amplify rather than diminish meaning.

This work suggests that apology transcends linguistic function to become corporeal language—a posture made visible, transforming abstract emotion into concrete typographic form.

Reductive Symbols

Medium: Print design, typography Year: 2024

About The Piece

This typographic symbol transforms the lowercase “n” into a bowed, humanlike figure—its posture evoking vulnerability, humility, and emotional sincerity. Created as a visual metaphor for the word apology, the design simplifies form to express feeling.

A tulip was added as a soft, symbolic gesture of compassion and outreach. Every curve and proportion references the original letterform, maintaining typographic integrity while expanding its emotional depth.

Apology becomes more than a word—it’s a posture, a feeling, a quiet gesture made visible through type.
