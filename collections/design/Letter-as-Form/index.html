Letter as Form

Medium: Typography, experimental design Year: 2025

Project Overview

Letter as Form investigates the metamorphosis of typographic characters from linguistic vessels to autonomous visual entities. This systematic exploration employs modular design methodology to demonstrate how letterforms can transcend semantic function and operate as pure structural elements within compositional space.

The Challenge

Transform familiar typographic characters into pure compositional elements, exploring how systematic design processes can defamiliarize common symbols and reveal their inherent formal qualities through strategic manipulation and modular organization.

Design Methodology

Working within rigid grid constraints, the project evolved through three distinct phases: character isolation studies, modular system development, and final compositional abstraction. Selected typefaces with pronounced sculptural qualities and high contrast ratios underwent systematic transformation through rotation, scaling, and layered relationships to generate rhythmic tension and directional momentum.

Outcome & Discovery

The resulting composition abstracts linguistic symbols into kinetic visual language, orchestrating viewer navigation through calculated variations in form weight and vectorial flow. Letter as Form demonstrates typography's capacity for defamiliarization—revealing how the most ubiquitous symbols can become estranged from meaning to achieve pure formal beauty.
