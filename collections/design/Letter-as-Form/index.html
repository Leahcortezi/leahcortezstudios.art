Letter as Form

Typography as Structure, Shape, and System

Course: GRDS 1302 Typography I Project: Problem 1 – Letter as Form Focus: Abstract Composition · Grid System · Formal Exploration

Project Overview

Prompt

Develop an abstract typographic composition using a 3x3 grid through three structured phases. The goal was to transform individual letterforms from linguistic symbols into expressive formal elements, ultimately creating a dynamic, unified composition rooted in form, contrast, and rhythm.

Phases:

  1. Phase 1: Create 50 unique 1" compositions using isolated letterforms in any orientation or scale
  2. Phase 2: Select 9 of those to group into 3x3 square compositions
  3. Phase 3: Refine one final composition for print using Adobe Illustrator

Concept & Design Strategy

This project explored the sculptural potential of type, emphasizing how letterforms can transcend legibility and become design elements in their own right.

I selected bold, curved letterforms with strong visual weight and contrast to maximize form-based impact. These were:

  • Rotated, scaled, cropped, and layered to form complex relationships
  • Arranged into interlocking patterns that emphasize rhythm and repetition
  • Carefully composed with positive/negative space to enhance flow and visual tension

The final composition balances visual density with structural clarity, using alignment and variation to guide the viewer's eye across the grid.

Visual Language

  • Grid System: Strict 3x3 square for modular structure
  • Letterform Choices: Emphasis on curves and boldness for high contrast
  • Tactics Used: Cropping, overlap, mirrored symmetry, vertical alignment
  • Formal Qualities: Sculptural, architectural, rhythmic, dynamic

Final Outcome

The final piece presents a bold typographic abstraction that explores form over function. Though the individual letterforms are unrecognizable in isolation, together they form a cohesive structure that pulses with tension, direction, and unity.

The composition is print-ready and optimized for black-and-white execution, reinforcing contrast and shape clarity.

Results & Reflection

This project taught me how to use typography as image, balancing freedom and constraint within a system. I refined my sensitivity to:

  • Spatial relationships
  • Tension between order and abstraction
  • Using rhythm and repetition as design principles
  • Structural consistency within modular design
