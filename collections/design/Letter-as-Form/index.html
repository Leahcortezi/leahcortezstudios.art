Letter as Form

Medium: Typography, experimental design Year: 2025

About The Piece

“Letter as Form” explores how typographic characters can transcend language to become expressive abstract shapes. This project challenged us to use only letterforms within a strict 3x3 grid system, developed across three phases—from single-letter compositions to a refined final design.

I selected bold letterforms with strong curves and sculptural potential, then manipulated them through cropping, rotation, layering, and scale. The final design emphasizes interlocking rhythm and visual flow, using a careful balance of positive and negative space to create tension and movement across the composition.

Though based on typography, the goal was not legibility—it was visual impact. The viewer’s eye is guided through the piece by variations in shape density, directional contrast, and the play between alignment and disruption.

“Letter as Form” turns language into structure, proving that even the most familiar symbols can become unfamiliar, sculptural, and beautifully ambiguous.
