Case Study

Letter as Form

Typography as Structure, Shape, and System

Abstract Composition Grid System Formal Exploration Typography I

Project Overview

Course

GRDS 1302 Typography I

Project

Problem 1 – Letter as Form

Challenge

Develop an abstract typographic composition using a 3x3 grid through three structured phases. Transform individual letterforms from linguistic symbols into expressive formal elements, creating a dynamic, unified composition rooted in form, contrast, and rhythm.

Approach

This project explored the sculptural potential of type, emphasizing how letterforms can transcend legibility and become design elements in their own right.

Typography as Visual Structure

Project Type: Experimental Typography & Formal Exploration
Focus: Abstract Composition · Grid System · Formal Exploration

Design Process

Three-phase approach to transforming letterforms into abstract visual elements

01

Phase 1: Exploration

Create 50 unique 1" compositions using isolated letterforms in any orientation or scale. This phase focused on discovering the sculptural potential of individual letters through rotation, cropping, and scaling.

02

Phase 2: Selection & Composition

Select 9 of the most successful compositions to group into 3x3 square arrangements. This phase emphasized creating relationships between forms and establishing rhythm across the grid system.

03

Phase 3: Refinement

Refine one final composition for print using Adobe Illustrator. Focus on perfecting the balance between visual density and structural clarity, optimizing for black-and-white execution.

Design Strategy

Creating unity through systematic formal exploration

Visual Language

  • Grid System: Strict 3x3 square for modular structure
  • Letterform Choices: Emphasis on curves and boldness for high contrast
  • Tactics Used: Cropping, overlap, mirrored symmetry, vertical alignment
  • Formal Qualities: Sculptural, architectural, rhythmic, dynamic

Design Strategy

I selected bold, curved letterforms with strong visual weight and contrast to maximize form-based impact. These were:

  • Rotated, scaled, cropped, and layered to form complex relationships
  • Arranged into interlocking patterns that emphasize rhythm and repetition
  • Carefully composed with positive/negative space to enhance flow and visual tension

The final composition balances visual density with structural clarity, using alignment and variation to guide the viewer's eye across the grid.

Final Outcome & Reflection

Typography as sculptural and architectural form

Final Outcome

  • Bold Typographic Abstraction: Explores form over function while maintaining structural unity
  • Sculptural Quality: Individual letterforms become unrecognizable yet cohesive building blocks
  • Print Optimization: Black-and-white execution reinforces contrast and shape clarity
  • Dynamic Composition: Creates tension, direction, and visual flow across the grid

Learning Outcomes

This project taught me how to use typography as image, balancing freedom and constraint within a system. I refined my sensitivity to:

Design Skills Developed

  • Spatial relationships and compositional balance
  • Tension between order and abstraction
  • Using rhythm and repetition as design principles
  • Structural consistency within modular design systems

Key Insight: Letterforms possess inherent sculptural potential that can be unlocked through systematic exploration, creating new visual languages that transcend traditional typographic conventions.
