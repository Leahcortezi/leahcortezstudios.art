Medium: Book cover design, typography Year: 2024

About The Piece

How to Be a Balloon Twister translates occupational methodology into typographic language, employing design systems to mirror the procedural nature of balloon artistry. This book cover investigation uses bold geometric frameworks, directional indicators, and abstracted forms to visualize concepts of movement, instruction, and material tension.

Constructed within a rigorous 3×3 grid system, the design balances structural control with expressive curvature that echoes inflated forms. Overlapping compositional elements, fragmented textual hierarchies, and directional arrows orchestrate visual navigation—emulating the pedagogical logic of instructional manuals and systematic creation processes.

This work functions as exercise in visual translation, where typography assumes the role of instruction itself. How to Be a Balloon Twister demonstrates how graphic design can encode professional knowledge, transforming abstract skills into concrete visual systems that communicate through rhythm, structure, and deliberate information architecture.
