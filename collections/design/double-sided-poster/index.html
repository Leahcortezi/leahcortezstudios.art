Double-Sided Poster

Medium: Print design, digital media Year: 2025

About The Piece

Commissioned for an exhibition examining political humor in early American discourse, this double-sided poster interprets Will Rogers' prescient observation: "Once ignorance gets started it knows no bounds." The work employs typographic methodology to investigate how misinformation systematically corrupts informational clarity.

The front panel eschews conventional presentation in favor of visual metaphor—oversized, fragmented slab serif letterforms and deliberately skewed hierarchical relationships manifest the chaotic proliferation of disinformation. These compositional distortions demonstrate how false information overwhelms cognitive processing, fracturing logical comprehension.

The reverse side extends this conceptual framework through strategic image cropping and misaligned columnar structures, while referencing Rogers' Western heritage through warm ochre palette and weathered typographic treatment. The dual-sided format creates dialogue between textual and visual information manipulation.

This work demonstrates how graphic design systems can function as sociopolitical commentary, using typographic disruption to embody the systematic distortion of truth. Through deliberate visual imbalance, the poster invites critical reflection on how design and humor can illuminate society's relationship with information integrity.
