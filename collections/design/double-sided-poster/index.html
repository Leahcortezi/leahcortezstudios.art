Double-Sided Poster

Medium: Print design, digital media Year: 2025

About The Piece

This double-sided poster was designed for an exhibition titled The Almost Right Word: Wit and Humor at the Dawn of Modern America, hosted by the Library of Congress. I selected a quote by Will Rogers—known as the “philosopher cowboy”—whose dry humor and political commentary exposed uncomfortable truths with striking clarity.

The front of the poster features a purely typographic interpretation of the quote “Once ignorance gets started it knows no bounds,” emphasizing the word “ignorance” by distorting its structure, misaligning letterforms, and disrupting spatial hierarchy. The oversized, fractured slab serif typography visually mimics the way misinformation overwhelms and distorts logic and structure.

The back of the poster continues this concept through fractured layout, cropped imagery, and misaligned text columns. It delivers Rogers’ biographical context while visually reinforcing the chaotic spread of misinformation. The design borrows influence from Rogers’ Western roots through a warm, earthy orange palette and typographic ruggedness, while also playing with grid tension and legibility.

This project explores how typographic systems can metaphorically embody societal issues—in this case, the distortion of truth. By using imbalance and fragmentation as visual devices, the poster invites viewers to reflect on how easily clarity can be manipulated, and how humor, when wielded thoughtfully, can be a powerful tool for critique.
