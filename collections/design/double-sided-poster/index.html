Double-Sided Poster

Medium: Print design, digital media Year: 2025

About The Piece

Created for an exhibition on political humor and early American wit, this double-sided poster interprets a quote by Will Rogers:
“Once ignorance gets started it knows no bounds.”

Rather than present the quote plainly, the piece visualizes how misinformation distorts truth. The front features a purely typographic treatment—oversized, fractured slab serif letterforms and skewed spatial hierarchy convey the chaotic nature of disinformation. The distorted layout emphasizes how misinformation overwhelms and breaks logic.

The back of the poster builds on this idea through cropped imagery and misaligned columns, reinforcing the message visually while drawing on Rogers’ Western roots with a warm orange palette and rugged typography.

This piece explores how typographic systems can metaphorically embody societal issues—here, the manipulation of clarity. Through imbalance and visual disruption, the poster invites viewers to reflect on how humor and design can critique the distortion of truth.
