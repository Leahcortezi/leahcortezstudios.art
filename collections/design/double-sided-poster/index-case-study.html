The Almost Right Word

Visualizing misinformation through typographic distortion

Challenge

Create a dual-sided poster that investigates misinformation's disruptive power through the lens of humorist Will Rogers. The design must visualize how "once ignorance gets started, it knows no bounds" while maintaining readability and conceptual clarity.

Approach

Using typographic distortion as a metaphor for information corruption, I created a poster where enlarged, skewed slab serif letterforms misalign and overflow, representing how misinformation destabilizes clarity. The dual-sided format contrasts structured information with fragmented chaos.

Conceptual Foundation

This poster critiques visual distortion while maintaining legibility, creating productive tension between clarity and chaos that mirrors our contemporary information landscape.

Design Process

From conceptual framework to visual execution

Conceptual Development

Explored how typography can embody the spread of misinformation. Developed the idea that distorted letterforms could represent how truth becomes corrupted as it spreads, while maintaining enough clarity to communicate the core message.

Typographic Distortion

Selected slab serif typography for its authoritative, news-like quality, then systematically distorted it through skewing, scaling, and misalignment. Each distortion represents a step away from factual information toward complete misinformation.

Dual-Sided Narrative

The front emphasizes the quote through bold, distorted typography. The back fractures biographical content into irregular columns and crops, referencing the loss of coherence that occurs when information systems break down. Western-inspired colors honor Rogers' cowboy persona.

Final Solution

A poster that teaches while it warns

Visual Impact

A dual-sided poster that uses typographic chaos as metaphor for information corruption.

  • Bold, distorted slab serif typography that maintains readability while suggesting corruption
  • Contrasting front and back that show information degradation
  • Western-inspired color palette honoring Will Rogers' heritage

Design Rationale

  • Typographic Distortion — Visual metaphor for how truth becomes corrupted
  • Dual-Sided Format — Contrasts clarity with fragmentation
  • Cultural Reference — Western aesthetics honor Rogers' cowboy persona
  • Readable Chaos — Maintains communication while demonstrating corruption

Results & Reflection

Project Outcomes:

  • Conceptual Success: Typography effectively embodies information corruption
  • Visual Impact: Creates immediate understanding of misinformation's effects
  • Cultural Relevance: Addresses contemporary concerns about truth and media
  • Design Innovation: Uses distortion as meaningful communication tool

Key Learnings:

  • Typography can be both message and metaphor simultaneously
  • Controlled chaos can enhance rather than hinder communication
  • Historical wisdom remains relevant to contemporary design challenges
  • Dual-sided formats create opportunities for narrative development
