Flag Design

Medium: Goachue Year: 2025 Size: 17"x11"

About The Piece

Flag Design investigates the semiotics of national identity through vexillological practice. This conceptual work for a fictional state employs reductive visual vocabulary—strategic color, geometric form, and hierarchical composition—to explore how complex cultural narratives can be distilled into powerful symbolic systems.

The tripartite structure utilizes horizontal banding to establish visual order while encoding specific meanings:

  • Red signifies protection and sovereign authority
  • Green represents agricultural abundance and economic prosperity

At the composition's center, a radiant sunburst functions as both unifying emblem and symbol of collective aspiration. Originally executed in gouache and subsequently digitized through Adobe Creative Suite, the work was selected for exhibition at the Mary and Jeff Bell Library, Texas A&M University–Corpus Christi.

This piece demonstrates how effective flag design transcends mere decoration to become a vessel for cultural storytelling—proving that the most profound meanings often emerge from the simplest forms.
