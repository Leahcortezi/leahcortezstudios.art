Flag of Elysia

A Color Theory Exploration of National Identity & Symbolic Paradise

Color Theory 2024

Concept

This flag design for the fictional tropical island nation of Elysia explores how color theory can communicate national identity and aspirational values. The goal was to create a symbolic vessel that distills cultural story and emotional geography into a cohesive visual identity.

Design Approach

The design employs two color theory principles: split complementary harmony for tropical vibrancy and simultaneous contrast to emphasize emotional warmth. The tripartite horizontal structure divides the flag into symbolic layers representing past, present, and future. The red field signifies protection and sovereignty, the green represents agricultural prosperity, and the central sunburst emblem unifies the design as a symbol of hope and forward momentum. Initial explorations were hand-painted in gouache to explore organic texture before digital refinement for scalability.

Result

The flag successfully functions at multiple scales while maintaining conceptual clarity. The design demonstrates how color theory and symbolic imagery can create meaningful national identity, balancing geographic essence with aspirational vision.
