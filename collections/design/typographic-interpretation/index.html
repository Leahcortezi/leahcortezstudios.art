About The Piece

HUNCH investigates intuitive cognition through typographic disruption, visualizing the pre-rational process of investigative insight. Rather than referencing physical posture, this work explores the detective's moment of subliminal recognition—when disparate clues coalesce into revelatory understanding before conscious thought can articulate the connection.

Employing a condensed sans serif foundation, the composition deliberately fractures typographic rhythm through strategic letterform interruption and evidentiary visual debris. These disruptions mirror the cognitive dissonance that accompanies intuitive breakthrough—the mental state where scattered information suddenly reorganizes into meaningful pattern.

HUNCH materializes the ephemeral moment of knowing before understanding, where instinct precedes logic. The work demonstrates how typography can function as psychological architecture, making visible the internal processes that occur in the liminal space between confusion and clarity.

Medium: Digital design, Typography Year: 2025

About The Piece

Instead of referencing “hunch” as a physical posture, this piece explores the intuitive kind—a detective’s gut feeling just before a breakthrough. The fragmented lettering reflects the internal chaos of suspicion, as clues and connections form beneath the surface.

Using a condensed sans serif typeface, I disrupted rhythm and structure with broken shapes and visual “evidence” to echo the messiness of instinct. The final composition captures that mental tension: scattered, erratic, yet leading somewhere meaningful.

HUNCH visualizes intuition as something you feel before you understand—a slow realization made visible through type.
