Among the Faithful

Medium: Printmaking Year: 2026 Dimensions: 5" × 7"

Project Overview

Among the Faithful is a personal exploration of what it feels like to practice religion while living in doubt. Growing up Catholic, I learned how faith can be both intimate and performative, where belief is expected to look certain even when it feels unstable. This piece sits in that conflict between devotion, guilt, and the constant pressure to appear convinced when internally you are still questioning.

At the center, a faceless hooded figure is caught in prayer. Removing the face became a way to show concealment and erasure at the same time, trying to belong while hiding what cannot be admitted out loud. Around that figure, repeated simplified bodies create a quiet sense of surveillance. Their expressions are intentionally unclear, somewhere between reverence and judgment, as if they can sense what the central figure is trying to mask.

The work is about the anxiety of being among the faithful while feeling disconnected from faith itself, the fear of being exposed as inauthentic, like a wolf in sheep's clothing. Instead of arguing for or against religion, the piece stays inside uncertainty. It holds the space where belief and doubt coexist and where spiritual longing can exist beside fear, contradiction, and unresolved questions.
