BIO

I'm Leah Cortez—a Chicana visual artist and BFA Communication Design student at Texas A&M University–Corpus Christi. This is a collection of work I've created over the years: images, objects, and ideas that have shaped how I see and how I make.

My work explores identity, memory, and meaning. I'm drawn to what feels sacred, forgotten, or fragmented—ritual, grief, family, faith. Whether through printmaking, digital design, or mixed media, I approach each piece as a form of storytelling, using texture, symbolism, and intuition to create something that feels true.

✦ MY PROCESS

Everything I make starts with feeling. An image that won't leave me, a memory that resurfaces, a question I can't answer. I work across mediums—digital, print, illustration, mixed media—because the idea determines the form, not the other way around.

I don't aim to provide answers. I'm more interested in opening up space for reflection, in evoking something quiet and familiar—like recalling a dream you never realized you had.

✦ ABOUT THIS ARCHIVE

This site is less a portfolio and more a personal archive—a living document of everything I've made and continue to make. It's organized not by what's "best" but by what feels honest to where I've been and where I'm going.

If something here resonates with you, or you'd like to talk about collaboration, feel free to reach out.
