Bio

Leah Cortez is a Chicana graphic designer and multimedia artist based in Corpus Christi, Texas. Currently studying Graphic Design at Texas A&M University–Corpus Christi, she explores themes of identity, spirituality, and the body through a gothic, symbolic, and surreal lens.

With a background in both traditional and digital media, Leah’s work blends intuitive mark-making, layered textures, and bold storytelling rooted in cultural memory and emotional truth. Her practice spans illustration, zines, web design, sculpture, and experimental mixed media—often combining elements like lace, anatomical motifs, religious iconography, and body horror.

Leah draws inspiration from her community, personal rituals, and the dreamlike space between decay and divinity. Through her art, she invites viewers to reflect on the intersections of heritage, emotion, and transformation.

Artist Statement

I create what I don't yet understand. My art is a way of reaching toward the unseen, toward emotions, memories, and questions that resist language. Each piece begins as an experiment: a visual problem to solve, a feeling to translate, or a memory to reconstruct. I work across digital and traditional media to explore the tension between clarity and ambiguity, using symbolic imagery and layered textures to express what cannot be said directly.

My work often engages with themes like religion, identity, existence, spirituality, and childhood trauma. I do not aim to provide answers, but rather to open up space for reflection and emotional truth. I am drawn to what is sacred, grotesque, forgotten, or fragmented. Through intuitive composition and symbolism, I try to evoke a sense of quiet recognition in the viewer, like recalling a dream you never realized you had.

In the end, my art is not about making clear statements. It is about creating a visual language for what haunts, heals, and hides beneath the surface.
