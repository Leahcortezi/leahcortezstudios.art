About Me

I'm Leah Cortez, a Chicana graphic designer and visual artist based in Corpus Christi, Texas. I create bold, expressive visuals that explore memory, identity, and emotion through a blend of graphic design and fine art. My work is rooted in storytelling — using design as a tool to communicate what words often can't.

With a BFA in Graphic Design and a minor in Studio Art from Texas A&M University–Corpus Christi, I've developed a practice that merges technical skill with intuitive, symbolic expression. Whether I'm working on personal projects or creative collaborations, I approach every piece with intention, curiosity, and a drive to create meaningful visual experiences.

I'm especially interested in projects that challenge perception, evoke feeling, and bring a strong conceptual voice to the forefront. If you're looking for design that's thoughtful, distinctive, and emotionally resonant — I'd love to connect.

My Creative Process

1. Discovery & Connection

I begin every project by deeply understanding your story, values, and goals. This includes exploring cultural context, target audience, and the emotional core of what you want to communicate.

2. Research & Inspiration

I gather visual references, cultural symbols, and conceptual frameworks that align with your vision. This research phase ensures authenticity and meaningful representation.

3. Concept Development

Using storytelling as my foundation, I develop multiple creative directions that balance artistic expression with strategic communication goals.

4. Collaborative Refinement

Through iterative feedback sessions, we refine the design together, ensuring it captures both your vision and resonates with your audience.

5. Final Delivery

I provide polished, professional files ready for implementation, along with guidance on how to maintain consistency across all applications.
