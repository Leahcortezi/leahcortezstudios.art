✦ BIO

I'm Leah Cortez, a Chicana visual artist and BFA Communication Design student at Texas A&M University–Corpus Christi, graduating in 2028. My work centers on cultural storytelling, symbolism, and emotional narrative—blending contemporary graphic design with personal and ritual-inspired imagery.

I'm drawn to projects that explore identity, memory, and meaning. Whether working digitally or through print-based processes, I approach design as a form of storytelling, using typography, imagery, and texture to create pieces that feel intentional, expressive, and grounded in story rather than trends.

✦ HOW I WORK

I create designs that begin with listening. Every project starts with understanding the story behind it—who it's for, what it represents, and what it needs to communicate. From there, I translate ideas into visuals that balance clarity with atmosphere, often incorporating symbolic elements, cultural references, and tactile qualities.

My practice spans digital design, printmaking, mixed media, and illustration, allowing me to adapt my approach to each project while maintaining a consistent visual voice.

✦ AVAILABLE FOR COMMISSIONS

I'm currently open to commissions, collaborations, and select client projects. I'm especially interested in working with individuals, creatives, and organizations seeking culturally grounded, story-driven design with a strong emotional presence.

If you have an idea—or even just a feeling—you'd like to explore visually, I'd love to help bring it to life.

Not sure how to describe what you're looking for? That's okay—we can figure it out together.
