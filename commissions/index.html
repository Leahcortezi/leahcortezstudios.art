Commission Intake

Ready to create something custom? Fill out this form to share your vision, budget, and timeline. I'll get back to you within 2–4 days to discuss the details and next steps.

✦ Quick Tip: The more details you share about your vision, reference images, and timeline, the better I can tailor my response to your needs. This helps us collaborate more efficiently.

Commission FAQ

What is your commission process?

Once you submit an inquiry, I'll respond within 2–4 days with more questions about your vision, a rate proposal, and a timeline. If we're a good fit, we'll agree on a contract and payment terms (typically 50% upfront, 50% upon completion). I'll send you sketches or comps for feedback before finalizing.

Can I see examples of your commissioned work?

Many of my commissions are confidential or under client agreements, so I can't always share them publicly. However, you can browse my archive to see my visual style, approach to different mediums, and the range of work I've done. If you'd like to discuss a specific type of project, I'm happy to share examples that are similar to what you're looking for.

What if I'm not sure about the details yet?

That's totally fine. Just give me as much context as you can—your gut feeling, reference images, the vibe you're going for. I love collaborating with people who are still figuring things out. My role is partly to help translate your ideas into visual form.

Do you work with tight deadlines?

It depends on my schedule and the scope of the project. Urgent commissions may require a rush fee. If you have a tight deadline, mention it in your inquiry and I'll let you know if I can accommodate it. I work best with timelines of at least 2–3 weeks, but let's talk.

What's your refund policy?

Commissions are non-refundable once work has begun, but I'm committed to making sure we're aligned every step of the way. If something isn't working, we'll iterate until it's right. If a major issue arises, we can discuss adjustments on a case-by-case basis.

Will I own the final work?

For most commissions, yes—you'll own the final deliverable. Usage rights and reproduction terms depend on the specific project and are outlined in the commission agreement. For example, if I create a logo for you, you own and can use the logo; if it's an illustration for editorial use, we'll define what that entails.
